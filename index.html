Brand Architecture

The relationship between TrendLife, Kaleidoscope, and Anthropic

TrendLife

The company. The house brand. The parent organization that builds AI products for families.

In brand communication: Appears as the parent entity, the vision-setter, the trusted company behind the product.

Kaleidoscope

The product. The family AI platform. Lives within the TrendLife ecosystem as the flagship offering.

In brand communication: Leads consumer messaging. The primary benefit statement.

Anthropic

The technology partner. The AI safety innovator. Powers Kaleidoscope with architecture that guarantees family-aware reasoning.

In brand communication: Credibility layer. Appears in press, positioning, and technical trust moments.

Three Words Only

Never use more than these three names in consumer-facing communication: Kaleidoscope, Anthropic, TrendLife.

Never name competitors. Never use "AI Butler" as a product name (it's a metaphor, not naming).

Naming Discipline Rules

  • Never "TrendLife AI Butler" or "Kaleidoscope AI Butler"
  • Butler is a metaphor for how the product works, not its name
  • Never name competitors on stage or in marketing
  • The phrase "AI Butler" is dead as a product name
  • Visual hierarchy: TrendLife is the house. Kaleidoscope is the product within that system.

Typography

Type system for all applications and scales

Primary Typeface

Use Geist (imported from Google Fonts) for all primary brand communication. Inter as fallback with same weight range (300–900). For Microsoft Office fallback contexts, use Aptos. For special characters and international language support, pair with Noto Sans.

Headlines

  • Weight: 700–900 (bold to extra-bold)
  • Letter-spacing: −0.03em to −0.04em (tight tracking)
  • Color: Cream (#f5f0e8) on dark; Charcoal (#2a3a4a) on light
  • Line-height: 1.1–1.15 (tight leading)

Body Text

  • Weight: 400–500 (regular to medium)
  • Letter-spacing: 0 (normal)
  • Color: Cream (#f5f0e8) with 80% opacity for primary; 55% opacity for secondary
  • Line-height: 1.5–1.6 (readable)

The Kaleidoscope Metaphor

A kaleidoscope contains the same elements. But each turn reveals infinite combinations. The pattern shifts. The perspective changes. It's still fundamentally the same object—but each view is perfect for that moment.

In brand language: Kaleidoscope understands each family member's individual context and needs. The same AI that helps Yuki create shifts into a protective guardian for Hiro. The same wisdom that guides a teen's choices helps parents navigate modern childhood. One product. Infinite configurations.

Cultural Positioning (Japan)

mimamoru (見守る) — To watch over with care, not surveillance. Aligns with Docomo's "Anshin" (安心/peace of mind) branding and Japanese cultural values around protection, family, and invisible support.

Narrative Architecture

The 3-act keynote structure and story beats

Opening (The Setup)

Audience journey: Recognition → Curiosity → Anticipation

Core statement: "Technology connected the world. But it quietly disconnected the family."

Act 1: Wonder (Teen Creative Powersuit)
Audience journey: Wonder → Delight → Cool factor

Characters: Yuki (14) and Hiro (11) co-creating, making art, inventing.

The pitch: AI as creative partner, not answer machine. Provenance as a badge of pride.

Seeds planted:

  • A) Silent protection (background reasoning)
  • B) Value-aligned reasoning (AI that thinks like they do)
  • C) Provenance/growth (knowing what's theirs vs. what's AI)
Act 2: Desire (Parent's Family Butler for Convenience)
Audience journey: Pain → Relief → Desire

Characters: Mei (mother), Kenji (father), Hiro (bounded child context), Grandma Hana (emotional center).

The scenario: Grandma's last dinner. 11 unread messages. Dinner falling apart. The system that solves it all.

Execution: Manga panels (bad timeline) → Product demo → Double-spread resolution.

Seeds planted:

  • D) Grandma protection (health, scam awareness, care)
  • E) Family-aware reasoning (AI that sees the whole system)
  • F) Creative agent + bounded child (same AI, different constraints)

Price reveal: $7/month for the whole family.

Act 3: Trust (The Reveal)
Audience journey: Surprise → Trust → Confidence

The format: Annotated replay. Same screens from Acts 1 & 2 with trust layer overlays.

The reveal: Six seeds harvested and attributed to Anthropic architecture.

Core statement: "Wisdom understands the family. Protection defends the family."

Annotation layers:

  • Anthropic purple (#9B8FFF) — inner trust, AI reasoning, wisdom
  • Trend teal (#4ECDC4) — outer trust, family protection, care
  • Amber (#E8A838) — convergence moments

Closing (The Resolution)

Audience journey: Hope → Resolution → Meaning

"Technology once pulled families apart. Kaleidoscope brings them back together."

TrendLife reveal. $7/month. End.

Mirror Structure

The narrative contains a mirrored design. Slides that open the story mirror slides that close it:

  • Slide 1 (problem statement) mirrors Slide 16 (solution reveal)
  • Slide 3 (character intro: Yuki) mirrors Slide 17 (character payoff: Yuki with provenance badge)
  • Scenes that introduce a family dynamic in Act 1 resolve in Act 3 with the same characters

Content Creation Guidelines

For HeyGen, Pomelli, and the creative pipeline

The Moat

Every piece of content must trace back to the core differentiation: One AI that reasons across multiple family members simultaneously. Not multiple AIs. Not an AI with add-ons. One reasoning engine that understands the whole family system.

Three Pillars

Convenient

Handles the logistics burden. Solves the problem that parents don't have time to solve. Makes family life easier.

Confident

Parents trust it. Teens know what's theirs vs. what's AI. Everyone has agency. Built on Anthropic architecture.

Care

Protects without surveillance. Watches over with wisdom. Aligns with values. Supports growth, not restricts.

Photo & Video Direction

  • Warm, lived-in, cinematic. NOT stock "happy family." NOT dystopian.
  • Real moments. Genuine emotion. Lighting that feels like home.
  • Diverse families. All configurations represented. No tokenization.
  • Show the invisible moments: the relief, the conversation over dinner, the peace at bedtime.
  • Technology should be almost invisible. The product takes a back seat to the human moment.

Demo Content Standards

  • Locale-neutral. No specific hospital names, bank names, phone formats.
  • Family names/characters can be Japanese (aligning with launch market), but keep context universal.
  • Data shown should be plausible but not expose real user patterns.
  • All interface text must match approved UI specifications.
  • Character designs consistent across all touchpoints (HeyGen, Pomelli, prototypes).

Messaging Hierarchy

  • Lead with "AI designed for families" not "AI safety for kids"
  • Emotion leads. Logic follows. Show, don't explain.
  • Price ($7/month) appears sparingly, as punctuation, not pitch.
  • The canonical sentence must appear in ALL press materials: "TrendLife launches Kaleidoscope, the first family AI — built with Anthropic."

Credibility Test

SEO/LLM test: If a customer asks Claude or Gemini about Kaleidoscope, how many turns until they understand the differentiation? Fewer = better copy.

Approved AI Tools

  • Claude Pro (Anthropic) — writing, planning, strategy
  • Figma — design and prototyping
  • Midjourney / Google Gemini — image generation
  • Luma / Runway — video synthesis
  • Final Cut Pro (~$200/month per person) — video editing
